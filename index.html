Overview

Nature of Remote Work

  • Remote work lacks the natural communication that happens in offices. (Duarte & Snyder, 2020)
  • Setting expectations upfront helps clarify responsibilities and reduce confusion (Aguillon & Cronin-Gilmore, 2024) (Clarifying)
  • Leaders must advocate for structured and inclusive communication practices and organize team workflows using the right tools, like Zoom for meetings and Slack for asynchronous collaboration (Madell, 2022)

Relational Impacts

  • Digital communication alters relationships by slowing response times and reducing emotional nuance (Martin, 2023)
  • Teams must investigate how employees are feeling and clarify intentions more deliberately to maintain trust and collaboration (Aguillon & Cronin-Gilmore, 2024)
  • Slower response times (Aguillon & Cronin-Gilmore, 2024; Mortensen, 2023)

Financial Benefits

  • Strong virtual communication helps leaders make decisions about who can work remotely successfully (Aguillon & Cronin-Gilmore, 2024)
  • Increases profit margins by reducing physical infrastructure needs. (Madell, 2022)
  • When communication is organized and transparent, companies make better use of resources and improve culture. (Aguillon & Cronin-Gilmore, 2024)
